The activity feed on your Sifa profile is opinionated. We do not show everything you do on the AT Protocol. We show the things that make sense in a professional context — work you created, not things you reacted to.

## What we show

- Articles you publish (WhiteWind, Smoke Signal long-form, and similar)
- Reviews you write (movies, books, etc.)
- Videos you upload
- Projects and other long-form artefacts you create

The exact list comes from an allow-list in our code. It changes as new AT Protocol record types appear. <!-- TODO: replace this paragraph with an auto-generated table from sifa-sdk once the registry is exported. -->

## What we leave out

- Likes
- Reposts
- Bookmarks
- Microblog posts (the ones that look like tweets)
- Comments and replies

The reason is not that those activities are unimportant. They just do not say much about your professional work, and if we showed them your profile would drown in noise. The Bluesky app already shows that side of you. We focus on the part that does not have a good home yet.

## Why it is set up this way

Most professional networks rank, score, or pad your profile with engagement metrics. We do not. Your profile is a record of work you did. If you want to know if someone is good at what they say they do, click through to the actual work.

## Want a different filter?

You cannot currently customise which categories show up on your profile. If there is a record type you think we should add — or remove — open an issue with a link to the lexicon and a short reason.
