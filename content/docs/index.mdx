Sifa is a professional network built on the AT Protocol. Your profile and activity are portable, your data lives on a Personal Data Server you can move, and your reputation comes from real work you publish — not from a score we made up.

This site explains the parts of Sifa that need more space than fits in the app.

## Getting started

- [Create a Sifa account](/docs/create-account) — sign in with an existing Atmosphere account, or create one
- [Import from LinkedIn](/docs/import-linkedin) — bring your positions, education, and skills across

## Atmosphere basics

For users new to the Atmosphere (the open network Sifa is built on):

- [What is the Atmosphere?](/docs/what-is-the-atmosphere) — the open network of apps that share one account system
- [DIDs, handles, and providers](/docs/dids-handles-providers) — the three parts of every Atmosphere account
- [Apps, AppViews, and the firehose](/docs/apps-appviews-firehose) — how the same data is read by multiple apps
- [Your data on a provider](/docs/your-data-on-a-provider) — where Sifa records actually live and how to inspect them
- [Lexicons and integrations](/docs/lexicons-and-integrations) — the open schemas that make this work

## Features

- [Activity feed](/docs/activity-feed) — what shows up on your profile and why
- [Atom feeds](/docs/atom-feeds) — subscribe to a Sifa profile from any feed reader, no account needed
- [Standard.site cards](/docs/standard-site-cards) — make your own site render as a rich card on Sifa, Bluesky, and other AT Protocol apps
