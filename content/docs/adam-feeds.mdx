Adam feeds are curated feeds on Sifa. Each one collects professional content on a theme — open source, climate work, design, whatever the curator picked. You can follow a feed and read it like you would read a timeline.

## How a feed gets its content

A feed has a curator. The curator picks the AT Protocol record types and accounts that qualify, and the feed shows whatever matches as it gets published. Curators do not have to be Sifa staff; anyone can build one.

For the technically curious: Adam feeds are AT Protocol feed generators. The protocol is open, so the same feeds can be consumed by other apps that speak feed generator. You are not locked in.

## Filtering: how to block content

You can hide content from a feed without unfollowing it.

- **Block an author.** Open their profile, hit the menu, choose Block. Anything they publish stops showing up in any feed you read on Sifa.
- **Mute a topic.** Open the feed's settings and add a keyword to the mute list. Posts whose text matches the keyword are hidden.
- **Hide a single post.** Use the post's menu and pick "Hide." Sifa remembers this for your account.

Blocks and mutes are stored on your account, so they follow you across devices.

## Why we have feeds at all

Your profile [activity feed](/docs/activity-feed) shows what _you_ publish. Adam feeds let you discover what other people publish. They are the discovery side of the same idea: professional content first, noise filtered out.
