When you share a link on Sifa or Bluesky, the app tries to render a preview card: title, description, image, author. The look of that card is controlled by your site, not by us. If you own the site, you can make the card say what you want it to say.

Sifa uses the [Standard.site](https://standard.site) spec for link metadata. It is the open card format adopted across the AT Protocol ecosystem.

## Why this matters on Sifa

On Sifa, the card you publish _is_ the marketing for your work. If you write articles on your own site instead of on a hosted platform, the card decides whether a casual reader bothers to click. A site without proper metadata renders as a bare URL on Sifa, Bluesky, and any other AT Protocol app — your work shows up looking like a stub.

## Where to start

If you have not done anything yet, start with the [Standard.site quickstart](https://standard.site). Then come back here for the Sifa-specific notes.
