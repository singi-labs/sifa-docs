---
title: Connect a Fediverse account
description: Link your Mastodon, GoToSocial, Pleroma, or other Fediverse account so your public posts show up in your Sifa activity feed.
---

If you post on Mastodon (or GoToSocial, Pleroma, Pixelfed, or any other Fediverse server), you can connect that account to Sifa ID and have your public posts appear in your [activity feed](/docs/activity-feed) alongside the rest of your Atmosphere activity.

## Connect your account

Open your profile, go to **External accounts**, and add an account with:

- **Platform**: pick **Fediverse**. This is the step that matters. If you add your Mastodon URL as a plain "Website", Sifa treats it as a link only and won't pull in your posts.
- **URL**: your profile address, for example `https://mastodon.social/@you`.

Save. Sifa displays the account as `@you@mastodon.social`, the handle format you're used to.

One more step is required before your posts are pulled in: you have to prove the account is yours (see [Verify that it's yours](#verify-that-its-yours) below). Until then, the account shows on your profile as a link only. This stops anyone from surfacing a stranger's posts on their own Sifa profile.

## What shows up

Sifa reads your account's public **outbox**, the standard ActivityPub feed of what you've published, and shows your posts in your activity feed.

- Only your **own posts** appear. Boosts, likes, and follows are left out, the same way Sifa never shows posts you liked on Bluesky.
- Only **public** posts. Followers-only posts and direct messages are never read.
- Posts sync **periodically**, not instantly. Expect your first posts to appear within an hour of verifying, then on an ongoing basis.

You can link **several** Fediverse accounts (say a Mastodon and a Pixelfed) and all of them ingest.

## A note on some servers

A few servers run in a hardened mode (Mastodon's "Secure mode", and GoToSocial by default) that limits what Sifa can read from the outside. If your posts don't appear after an hour, your server may be one of these. We're expanding support for them.

## Verify that it's yours

Verifying ownership is **required** before Sifa ingests your posts (it also puts a verified badge on the link). Use either method:

- **`rel="me"` back-link.** Add your Sifa profile URL to your Fediverse profile so your server publishes it with `rel="me"`. Sifa fetches your profile, finds the matching `rel="me"` link, and verifies the account. Where the URL goes matters (see below).
- **[Keytrace](https://keytrace.dev).** If you verify the account through Keytrace, Sifa detects it automatically, no back-link needed. This is the simplest route.

Until one of these passes, the account still appears on your profile, but only as a link, and none of its posts are pulled in.

### Adding the `rel="me"` link

The link has to go in a **profile field**, not your bio. Bio links are published as `rel="nofollow noopener"`, without the `me` part, so they never verify. This is the usual reason a link "won't turn into `rel="me"`".

- **Mastodon:** Preferences -> Profile -> **Profile metadata**. Add a row, for example name `Sifa` and content `https://sifa.id/p/<your-handle>`. Mastodon publishes profile-metadata links with `rel="me"` automatically.
- **GoToSocial / Pleroma / Akkoma:** add the URL in your profile's fields / metadata (the same key-value fields Mastodon uses).
- **Pixelfed:** add the URL in your profile's website or links field.

You do not need your own server's green "verified" checkmark. That checkmark is bidirectional (it also wants a link back from the target), but Sifa only needs the one-way `rel="me"` from your account to your Sifa profile.
