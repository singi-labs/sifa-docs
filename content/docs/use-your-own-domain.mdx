---
title: Use your own domain as your handle
description: Replace `alice.bsky.social` with `alice.com` — your own domain becomes your Atmosphere identity. The setup lives on your provider (or via DNS), not on Sifa.
---

By default, your Atmosphere handle ends in your provider's domain — `alice.bsky.social` if you're on Bluesky, `alice.eurosky.social` on Eurosky. You can replace that with your own domain (`alice.com`, `me.example.org`) so your professional identity isn't tied to a provider's name.

The setup happens at the **AT Protocol level**, on your provider. Sifa does not offer this as a hosted service — we don't run a whitelabel handle service. What we offer is the profile that uses whatever handle you've configured upstream.

Once your handle is set, your Sifa profile URL automatically becomes `https://sifa.id/p/alice.com`. No further configuration needed on Sifa.

## What you need before you start

- An Atmosphere account on any provider (Bluesky, Eurosky, etc).
- A domain you control.
- Either the ability to add DNS TXT records to that domain, or the ability to serve a single file at `https://<your-domain>/.well-known/atproto-did`.

If you control DNS but not `.well-known/`, use the DNS method. If you control `.well-known/` (most hosting platforms) but not DNS, use the file method. Either works.

## Step 1 — find your DID

Your DID is the permanent ID for your account. You need its value to point your domain at it.

In the Bluesky app: **Settings → Account → Advanced → Decentralised Identity (DID)**. Copy the long string that starts with `did:plc:`.

In Sifa: open your profile page and look in the settings panel.

From the command line:

```bash
curl -s "https://bsky.social/xrpc/com.atproto.identity.resolveHandle?handle=<your-current-handle>" | jq -r .did
```

Substitute your provider's hostname if you're not on Bluesky.

## Step 2 (option A) — set a DNS TXT record

On your DNS provider (Cloudflare, Hetzner, Route 53, your registrar's panel — wherever you manage records for the domain):

- **Type:** TXT
- **Name:** `_atproto.<your-domain>` (so for `alice.com`, the name is literally `_atproto.alice.com`. Some DNS UIs ask you to enter just `_atproto` and they append the domain automatically — both are correct.)
- **Value:** `did=did:plc:<your-did-here>`
- **TTL:** 300 is fine.

Save. The record propagates in a few minutes.

Verify:

```bash
dig +short TXT _atproto.alice.com
# should return: "did=did:plc:abc123..."
```

## Step 2 (option B) — serve a `.well-known/atproto-did` file

If you can't set DNS TXT records (managed hosts sometimes block this), serve a plain-text file at:

```
https://<your-domain>/.well-known/atproto-did
```

The file's contents are a single line: your DID. Nothing else.

```
did:plc:abc123def456...
```

The response must be served with `Content-Type: text/plain` and respond to HTTPS. Most static hosts (GitHub Pages, Netlify, Cloudflare Pages, plain nginx) let you serve files at any path. Ghost Pro, some managed WordPress, and a handful of SaaS site builders block `/.well-known/` — in that case you must use the DNS method or move hosts.

Verify:

```bash
curl -s https://alice.com/.well-known/atproto-did
# should print: did:plc:abc123...
```

## Step 3 — update your handle on your provider

With the DNS or well-known set, tell your provider you want to change your handle.

**On Bluesky:** Settings → Account → Handle → I have my own domain. Enter `alice.com`. The Bluesky app runs the verification; if it sees your DNS record or fetches your well-known file successfully, the handle is updated.

**On Eurosky / Tangled / Blacksky:** roughly the same flow under Settings or Account.

If the verification fails, double-check the steps above. DNS sometimes takes a few minutes to propagate; if `dig +short TXT _atproto.<domain>` returns the right value but your provider says no, wait 5 minutes and retry.

## Step 4 — verify on Sifa

Sign out of Sifa and sign back in with your new handle. Your profile page is now `https://sifa.id/p/alice.com`.

Old links pointing at `https://sifa.id/p/alice.bsky.social` continue to resolve — the AppView indexes by DID, not handle, so any path that maps to your DID renders the same profile.

## Why do this

- **Independence from your provider.** If you ever move from Bluesky to Eurosky, your domain handle stays. People who linked to `alice.com` don't lose anything.
- **Personal-brand surface.** A short, memorable handle that matches your website. `sifa.id/p/alice.com` reads as your CV the same way `alice.com` reads as your blog.
- **It's a one-time setup.** DNS records and well-known files don't decay. Set once, runs for years.
