If Sifa is your first Atmosphere app, this page is for you.

The **Atmosphere** is an open network of apps that all speak the same protocol — the [AT Protocol](https://atproto.com). The name "Atmosphere" is the friendly label; "AT Protocol" is the technical one. They mean the same thing.

What makes the Atmosphere different from a regular social platform: every app shares **one account system**. When you create an Atmosphere account with any provider — Bluesky, Eurosky, Tangled, Blacksky, or any of the others — that same account works on **every other Atmosphere app**. Sifa included.

You log in to Sifa with the account you already have, or one you create somewhere else. Sifa does not run its own login.

## What you get from being on the Atmosphere

- **One account, many apps.** Sign in once, use Sifa, Bluesky, Tangled, Leaflet, Smoke Signal, and however many more launch this year. No re-registering, no fragmented identity.
- **Your data is yours.** Your career history, your posts, your follows — everything lives in your account, not in Sifa's database. If Sifa shuts down tomorrow, your profile keeps working in any other app that reads the same records.
- **Switch providers without losing anything.** The company storing your account today doesn't have to be the same one storing it next year. You can move and your followers, posts, and history come with you.
- **Apps you've never heard of can render your work.** Build a personal dashboard, a custom CV generator, a long-form reader — they can all read your Sifa profile because the data is publicly accessible through the protocol.

## The trade-off

Open networks are slightly more work to understand than walled gardens. Bluesky abstracts most of this away, and so does Sifa for the common cases, but the model is different from "sign up with email and password":

- You have a **handle** (like `alice.bsky.social` or `alice.com`) instead of just a username.
- You have a **provider** (sometimes called a PDS — Personal Data Server) where your account data lives.
- You have a **DID** (a permanent identifier) that follows you around even if your handle or provider changes.

Don't worry if those terms feel abstract right now. The next pages walk through each one.
