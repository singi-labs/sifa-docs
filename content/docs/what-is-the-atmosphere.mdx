If Sifa is your first Atmosphere app, start here.

The **Atmosphere** is an open network of apps that all speak the same protocol: the [AT Protocol](https://atproto.com). "Atmosphere" is the friendly label, "AT Protocol" is the technical one. Same thing.

What makes the Atmosphere different from a regular social platform: every app shares **one account system**. Create an Atmosphere account with any provider (Bluesky, Eurosky, Tangled, Blacksky, or any of the others) and that same account works on **every other Atmosphere app**. Sifa included.

You log in to Sifa with the account you already have, or one you create somewhere else. Sifa doesn't run its own login.

## What you get

- **One account, many apps.** Sign in once, use Sifa, Bluesky, Tangled, Leaflet, Smoke Signal, and however many more launch this year. No re-registering, no fragmented identity.
- **Your data is yours.** Career history, posts, follows: it all lives in your account, not in Sifa's database. Sifa shuts down tomorrow and your profile keeps working in any other app that reads the same records.
- **Switch providers without losing anything.** The company storing your account today doesn't have to be the same one storing it next year. You move, your followers, posts, and history come with you.
- **Apps you've never heard of can render your work.** A personal dashboard, a custom CV generator, a long-form reader: all of them can read your Sifa profile because the data is publicly accessible through the protocol.

## The trade-off

Open networks take a bit more to wrap your head around than walled gardens. Bluesky hides most of this, and so does Sifa for the common cases, but the model is different from "sign up with email and password":

- You have a **handle** (like `alice.bsky.social` or `alice.com`), not just a username.
- You have a **provider** (sometimes called a PDS, Personal Data Server) where your account data lives.
- You have a **DID** (a permanent identifier) that follows you around even when your handle or provider changes.

If that feels abstract, don't worry. The next pages walk through each piece.
